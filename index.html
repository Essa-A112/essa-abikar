About

I am a mechanical engineer by training with a strong foundation in quantitative methods, machine learning, and data-driven decision making. My MSc thesis at King's College London, which earned a distinction, focused on AI-powered investment decision support using machine learning with an emphasis on model explainability.

I co-authored peer-reviewed research on thermodynamic modelling of high-temperature heat pump systems, published in Springer conference proceedings. I have also applied engineering thinking in industry through my work at Kirfu, an AI logistics startup.

I volunteer with Engineers Without Borders and the Haya Community Centre, applying technical skills to community development. I am actively pursuing roles in data science, quantitative finance, and financial engineering.
